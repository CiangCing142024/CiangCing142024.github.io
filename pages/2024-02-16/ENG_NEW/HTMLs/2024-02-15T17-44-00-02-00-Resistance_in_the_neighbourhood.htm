The river of rage has overflowed, the tractors go down the streets, blocks have been set up all over the country with farmers rising against policies that want to destroy them. Breeders, farmers, beekeepers, the whole of the poor and medium -sized peasantry are struggling for their survival. Floods and disasters, prices of their products that are mismatches with production costs and anti -rational policies constantly squeeze farmers' income down. The government announcements and crumbs that the prime minister put in his meeting with them cannot equalize the disaster that thousands of rural households have suffered. Everything shows that these constantly thickens are not for the "price of weapons". There are mobilizations that result from the accumulated indignation of the peasantry. The same indignation is expressed by the farmers of the whole of Europe who are on the road with common problems and joint claims.


We students who have resolved in a racing orbit must jointly build a common front of resistance with the peasantry and the people to overthrow the policy that is moving a new hit of our rights. Our occupations that continue and our mass demonstrations must meet with blocks and agricultural mobilizations. The government will be even more squeezed when the struggles of students, farmers and workers are coordinated and together fight for their work, crop and studies.

Common problems, common and our enemies:

What breaks up, government and ministries respond with terrorism and repression. Matt and wood in the schools, as well as in the farm block, prosecutors for those who close the streets and for those who occupies. The agrarian and disciplinary industry warms its machines once again. The intensity of fascism reveals the panic of the government in front of the new wave of mobilization.

The attack we receive students and farmers has a common source and this is none other than the policies that serve the interests of the local and foreign capital. They are the policies of the EU and its mechanisms that, with the "European Education" and the OECD, hit our right to studies and with "Common Agricultural Policy" deprive the right of farmers to live by working on their land. We have to confront them!

Our only choice is the common struggle with the people, the peasantry and the workers in the struggle against the policies that want to level our rights.
