In a few hours Missak Manouchian and his wife Mélinée will be buried in the Pantheon, a Parisian monument where the "figures of the history of France" rest.
Missak and her twenty-three comrades, Jews, Spanish republicans, stateless have not just fought for France "they fought against Hitler's fascism like all the resistance fighters but have this central feature of being all communist activists who have Makes the greatest sacrifice, the highest by giving their lives for the people.
Missak and his comrades prepared the field, pouring their blood as tens of thousands of members of the Glorious Communist Party of France, for the conquest of social achievements of 1945, retirement or social security.

What an unignable hypocrisy when you know with what violence and determination the bourgeoisie attacked these great conquests paid by blood among other things of the red poster.

Macron and his clique have nothing to do with Missak Manouchian and the red poster. This tribute is only an insult, a provocation, a spit made by the bourgeoisie on the grave of the heroes of the resistance.

Missak and her comrades have no place in a bourgeoisie pantheon, their bodies have nothing to do alongside those of Napoleonic marshals or Jean Monnet: Missak, Mélinée and the others have devoted their lives to shoot down the bourgeoisie, to organize the socialist revolution. The memory of our comrades will not be honored through a ceremony projected on the television channels of bourgeois media monopolies, they are honored on a daily basis by the proletarian youth of France, their names resonate in the updates of June and in the daily actions of the worthy heirs of the youth battalions.

Missak, the glorious fighters of the red poster and all the others live in the ardent struggle of the French proletariat, so let's never leave the bodies of our comrades flouted by the worst reactionaries!

80 years later we remember the sacrifice of our comrades and we always raise the red flag of those who were " Lovers to live to die from it ”, We assert with certainty that their bodies will rest in socialist France sooner or later for which they gave their lives.
