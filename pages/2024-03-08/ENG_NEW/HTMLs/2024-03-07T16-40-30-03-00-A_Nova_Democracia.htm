The death of the young officer and the commitment that guided it seems very much to those of another brave and altruistic American who, in 2003, at the age of 23, delivered his life fighting in a non -violent way against the crimes of Zionism: Rachel Corrie. It is surprising, and only explained by the damage that the incessant overlap of images and stimuli causes memory and reasoning that it and this obvious resemblance have not been remembered immediately.

Admittedly, Corrie did not die in her own hands, but kicked by the Israeli army by placing herself between the house of a Palestinian family and the military excavator that would demolish her. But differences weigh less than background similarities.

The largest of them: the decision, common to both, to put the convictions and justice above life itself. Such a force led Bushnell to go resolutely toward death and Correi accept it as a possibility by exposing himself to Zionist cowardice and cruelty. He was sure of his end and consciously sought him; She knew she risked, not because Israel's army was legitimate running over her, but because she knows that he acts without sticking to criteria of justice, morals or legitimacy.

To say this is not, as it is commonly said, to blame the victims: it is to recognize the heroes. Bushnell and Corrie are not victims, they are heroes.

3

It shocks and hurts to think of such young and valuable lives so early. Those who are able to surrender thus to a righteous cause would be able to, alive, do many valuable things.

The sense of injustice and something out of place is also inevitable; Bushnell and Corrie should be alive, and Zionist criminals should be dead.

However, they soon give way to the understanding that some (rare) times a life finds their fulfillment in their own sacrifice. And that both have achieved - much more than most of those we remain alive - to establish “the noble pact between the bloody cosmos and the pure soul,” in the words of the author of the epigraph.

Among the lessons that the heroic acts of both teach or remembers, it is to die of old in a bed is not always the best ending a human being can long.

4

Both gave life in acts of peaceful resistance: Corrie has committed no act of violence against someone, Bushnell only against himself.

This is not opposed to armed and/or violent actions: Bushnell himself, as a military man, had assumed the possibility of killing in a war as much as to die.

Pacific resistance is not the opposite of armed resistance, with which it has a common denominator expressed in a common word requires as much conviction, action and commitment as it is, and is closer to it than the warm policy-political policy that unfolds to the base of conchavos in cabinets with air conditioning.

Nor should it be confused with the pretense of equidistance that washes his hands equating the Zionist colonizer who has already exhausted the catalog of war crimes and against humanity, the Palestinian National Resistance and his armed actions - recognized, in addition, as legitimate by International Law .

The method chosen by Bushnell and Corrie is not replicable on a large scale: under exceptional circumstances, it can be of great value, and they dignified it.

5

The meaning of their sacrifices, however, is only complete through the action of those who remain alive. Their gestures denote full confidence in humanity and challenge it.

His luminous examples call us a greater commitment and serve to raise consciousness and fight to another level. The reactions of admiration and wrath against the crimes of Israel that awaken worldwide, starting with the country that saw them born, show that the point of non-rector for Zionism approaches, and that their sacrifices will not have been in vain .

May the roots attached by them in our consciences be firm and deep.
