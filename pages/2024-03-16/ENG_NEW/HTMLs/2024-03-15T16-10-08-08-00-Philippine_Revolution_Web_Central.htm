2) What is the "Strategic Island Defense" of the AFP?

The US, AFP and Marcos' officials have been joining the AFP's "turning" from the "internal defense" to the "external defense" because the New People's army is "weak" or "overwhelmed". At the beginning of the year, Gilbert Teodoro, Secretary of the Department of National Defense, announced the formation of the AFP's Comprehensive Archipelagic Defense Concept (CADC). This defense plan is allegedly for protection of the Philippine exclusive economic zone in Palawan (where the presence of American and Chinese ships), and in Batanes (which are planned to be the US headquarters because of its approach to Taiwan).

In fact, the CADC is associated with the US "Archipelagic Defense Strategy" that has pushed for the laying of the system of missile and cannon in its so-called "first-island chain." This strategy is promoted as a way to prevent the expansion of the "threat" of China's military power. Behind this strategy is giving the US mainland a "deep defense" when the war broke out between it and China. It is a major part of the US's north and western Philippines as military bases and facilities that will serve as a lover of the mysterious attack that will fall inside the mainland of China.

4) What other agreements does the US use to maintain its presence in the country?

On May 1, 2023, Ferdinand Marcos Jr and Joseph Biden, US president, signed the Bilateral Defense Guidelines, a document re-enacting the 1951 Mutual Defense Treaty (MDT), a colonial treaty that tied the Philippines to US foreign policy. In a US-prepared "guide," it set out funding, training and arming the AFP to serve the puppet army in its anti-China objectives. In April 2023, the Philippines signed a 5-10-year security plan for the "modernization" of AFP weapons, camps and facilities.

Since the MDT, it has served as a basis for the US invasion of the internal national issues and dragging the Philippines into the wars that the US has entered in the past seven decades.

In 1947, the US pushed for the Military Bases Agreement (MBA) and used 65,000 hectares of agricultural land and ancestral land to serve as a base for American soldiers, and storage of its weapons, including nuclear weapons. These bases have been turned into a starting point for US aggression forces in these wars.

The MDT served as a destination for other military agreements, especially after the Philippine Senate dismissed the MBA in 1992. Among them are:

Visiting Forces Agreement: 1998, signed by the Ramos regime. Allowed free discharge and stay of large numbers of American troops, and gave them extrateritorial rights.

In 2006, the US developed the Security Engagement Board (SEB), based on the VFA, to set AFP's "community" activities and easier to manage large Wargames such as Balikatan. Also through Seb, it is determined what the Philippine weapons "buy" in the US, under the US Foreign Military Funding program.

Enhanced Defense Cooperation Agreement: 2014, signed by the Aquino II regime. It allowed the construction of exclusive US military facilities and bases within AFP military camps to use them as a dump of US military equipment, or the rest of American troops in the country.

This is now the basis for building military bases wherever the US wants in the country.

The roots of the US and China, which began to intensify in the mid-2000s, were economical. Since the end of the 1970s, the US has supported China's continuous transformation from socialist to being capitalist, including monopoly bureaucrat capitalists in China. The US has sparked China with trade and investment (“most favored nation”). It allows China to develop its own industry in conjunction with the growing part of production, especially in the field of technology.

The monopoly-capitalists of the state in China benefit the offensive of neoliberalism or liberalization, deregulation, and privatization in China and around the world. China quickly transformed into a newly emerged imperialist power. The monopoly-capitalists in China have exported capital in the form of loans and investments. China has repeatedly strengthened its military forces and its overseas operations. These include the construction of military bases on seven artificial islands in the South China Sea and within the Philippine maritime territory, to control the trade route here.

The construction of these military bases is an explicit military aggression and is against the Philippine naval rights recognized by the International Arbitral Tribunal's decision under the United Nations Convention on the Laws of the Seas (UNCLOS). During the economic era, the US renewed this China's strengthening.

Following the financial crisis in 2008, the US complained of its trade in China, China's control over a major part of the global trade. The US has pushed for the formation of trade blocks that settled in China, in conjunction with the implementation of protective policies. In 2011, the US government declared the "Pivot to Asia" to rotate the focus of its military forces from Europe and the Middle East to Asia.

Under the Trump government to the Biden government, the US imposed on the pressure on the Chinese economy, allegedly to punish it for not complying with the "global principles." The US also imposed pressure on importing agricultural products from China.
