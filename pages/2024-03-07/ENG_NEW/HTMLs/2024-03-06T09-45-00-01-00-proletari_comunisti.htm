A communist commitment .
In Asking in 1933 his naturalization which will remain without follow -up, Missak Manouchian shows his attachment to France, he will renew his request for naturalization in 1940 which was refused again.
Missak Manouchian, Armenian poet and resistant, was born 1906 in a family Armenian peasants in Türkiye. His family dies in the genocide Armenian [1] perpetrated by Turkey. He arrived in France in 1924 at the age of 9.
He exercises a carpenter profession in Marseille, then is hired as Tourner at Citroën factories in Paris. Passionate about literature Armenian and French, he frequented libraries, takes courses as a free listener at the Sorbonne and founded in 1930 a review in Armenian Tchank (In Armenian: ջանք, literally "effort"), his publication ceased in June 1931 after 12 issues.
Missak Manouchian joined the Communist Party the day after the demonstration antiplacing on February 6, 1934, led by the extreme leagues RIGHT [2] . He also joined the French section of the rescue committee of Armenia, an organization linked to the Communist International aimed at Mobilize the Armenian diaspora in favor of the Soviet Republic Armenia.
HAS The declaration of war in 1939 Missak Manouchian was arrested and Imprisoned but released for lack of charge and will live in hiding. His state status and communist commitment (the Communist Party will be dissolved a few weeks later) place in the category of "Unwanted" that the Daladier government wants to see.
He was interned at the end of June 1941 at the Royallieu camp in Compiègne by the Germans who then carry out preventive arrests in Communist environments, in the context of the Barbarossa operation [4] . Released for lack of charges, Missak Manouchian resumes his activity activist and integrates the immigrant labor (me), a structure created by the Communist Party to supervise immigrants and refugees arrived In France at the end of the First World War. He becomes the Political Manager of the Armenian section between the end of 1941 and the At the beginning of 1942 under the pseudonym of « Georges » .

Combatants in busy Paris
In February 1943, he was paid in the clandestine organization Small and supporters-migrant work (FTP-MOI-BRANCHE military of the self), then the only group resisting to carry out actions armed against the occupier in Paris. He participated in his first armed action on March 17, 1943 in Levallois-Perret by attacking the Granada a group of German soldiers. He is appointed commissioner soldier of the Paris region in August 1943, having under his orders About fifty activists. Groups under his command lead Thirty armed operations in August in mid-November 1943, whose execution on September 28 of the German general Julius Ritter, responsible in France for the requisition of labor within the framework compulsory labor service (STO).
THE reprisals are deadly (fifty hostages shot on October 5 At Mont-Valérien), the response of the resistance fighters is just as much: 15 Attached causing large losses to the Germans.
Missak Manouchian was arrested in the Paris region on November 16, 1943 by the special French police brigades (3) After a long spin, when he had an appointment with the chief Interregional of FTP Joseph Epstein. In the days that follow, the FTP-Me groups are massively arrested and delivered to the Germans. They are tortured, none speak to protect their comrades.
