Reaping future

Dawn in the deep Chaco. There, in the then ingenuity Las Palmas del Chaco Austral, where in 1885 the electric light was turned on for the first time in our country, today it is distinguished by something innovative: agroecological cotton occurs, under community organization and that returns in supplies For the elaboration of newborn clothing.

Little by little, they are filled. Then the white flakes accumulate on a canvas to dry under the sun. There is enthusiasm: it seems that this year the quality and quantity is exceeded.

The “No’Oxonec- border cotton” project

The project is called “No’Oxonec-border cotton” -which means "fabric" in Qom-, and the CCC of Las Palmas is a link more than a chain of social and solidarity economy. It began in 2020, based on the “Productive Alliances” program of the socio -economic inclusion project in rural areas (stepping) funded by the World Bank. The prominence is the Creole families and belonging to the Qom people of Pampa del Indio, Qochiñilae (Roca Presidency), the Leonese and Las Palmas. And, also, numerous organizations and spaces participate such as the Dr. Ramón Carrillo Foundation, the Association of Small Producers of the Chaco, the CCC of Las Palmas, the National Campesina Federation of the Maipú Rural Place Different rural consortiums, cooperatives and textile workshops, such as the "interwoven collective" and "Kalu Gryb", both of resistance, among others. In addition, it has the Technical Assistance of the National Institute of Agricultural Technology (INTA), the National Institute of Industrial Technology (INTI) and the help of the municipalities of Presidency Roca, Pampa del Indio and Las Palmas, the Undersecretariat of Agriculture, the Undersecretariat of cotton and the Institute of Culture of the Province of Chaco.

The objective is to rebuild the productive system in communities affected by fumigations with agrotoxic through the production of agroecological cotton. It is done taking advantage of the goodness of the soil, with natural supplies and fertilizers and it is aspired to reach an added value that is 100% Chaqueño. The reference of producing in a healthy way is not a minor fact for the "Chacra Group", as members of the Social Movement, responsible for planting and care tasks. The reason that makes the difference is the experience with the contamination of the Rroceras San Carlos S.R.L. and Court Long S.A., who for years became synonymous with transgenics and agrotoxic, until a precautionary measure managed to impose a limit on air fumigations. This is reported by a young man from the group:

“Here do not use chemicals that will damage the earth. Before we had the problem of the rice that long chemicals and there were seasons that fish, plants and grabbed diseases that you went to the hospital were dying and did not know what you had. After they stopped doing it, the plants are seen to grow more strongly, it is not as before. ”

The seed is the germ of this project. They are not transgenic, that means that they have not been genetically modified and that they can be reproduced naturally, work that is consolidated after each harvest. Once the cotton is taken to disassemble, the fiber is separated and the seeds return to the farm for storage and the same is solidarily distributed in neighboring communities.

To start the sowing tasks, who integrate the CCC Chacra group, they also received advice and training in agroecology, which provided INTA technicians and the Dr. Ramón Carrillo Foundation. This education is very important because it allows to combine the knowledge of those who have experience in cotton production, tradition by antonomasia in the Argentine Chaco, and the knowledge of agroecology, which promotes more sustainable and resilient systems. From here, tasks arise for the elaboration of natural fertilizers and pesticides where other agrodive productions such as orchards, bean, pumpkin, watermelon and agroecological corn are also developed.

And well, going to look for those posts is to put the workforce of companions in places where they allow us to enter to look for palm trees. You have to walk, far, each with their water and something to eat, all day. Some carry an ax, other chainsaw, which carries its oil and fuel, that if the leaf is broken it has a tremendous cost. They are very hard days and days; 100, 200, 300 posts. Then bring them, do the wells and get the wire and the cost of wire! It is one thing to tell it and another thing is to see it. ”

Thats not all. The weather also played a bad pass. In October last year, a strong storm with hail drop ruined 40% of sown cotton, 70% corn, pumpkin and watermelon and 100% leafy vegetables, together with the destruction of the infrastructure. The Chakra group had to sow and cope with the adversity of starting again.

The "Booty of War", according to Javier Milei

On March 1, the President of the Nation accused the left -wing organizations of using social programs such as "Botín de Guerra" that steal silver and attentive to those who are beneficiaries of that right. This defamation of the National Government for stigmatizing social organizations and movements is not new and intends to create a scenario of delegitimization and violence to remove assistance and further impoverish popular sectors.

The reality is another: we have seen that it is the social movements that guarantee popular pots in times of food emergency, vaccination in times of pandemic and organization when social assistance arrives; They maintain and organize territorial work in every corner of Argentina. In short, the community experience of agroecological cotton and many others that can be shown, places them as the true guarantors that public policies arrive and concretize in the territories, even in the difficult working conditions and availability of resources. This is what makes them target stigmatizations and persecutions. However, in the face of these unfounded sayings, by force of struggle and commitment, social movements in Chaco redouble the commitment and harvest future, promoting proposals for the development of agriculture with farmers, of the national industry and regional economies.
