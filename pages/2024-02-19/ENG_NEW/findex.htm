The revolutionary and member in memoriam From the editorial board of the New Democracy newspaper, Alípio de Freitas, was honored on February 17 at an event organized by the AJA Lisbon Center in the Portuguese capital. The day marks Alipio's birthday, which would have turned 95. The Poor Peasant League (LCP) of Brazil, which had Alipio as President of Honor, sent a message to the celebration, announcing that the revolutionary will be honored in several honors of workers and peasants of Brazilian lands. The note can be read in full at the end of the matter.

The message of the peasant organization was devoted to Alipio's wife, Guadalupe Portelinha. “Here in Brazil, the companions of the Belo Horizonte Construction Workers Union (popularly known for over 35 years by the name Marreta, the organization of workers who resumed the union's direction for the class expelling the interveners of the military dictatorship), and Also the Labor League, which together have one of the most heard radio programs in the capital of Minas Gerais, will pay tribute, which will be accompanied by peasants across the country in the revolutionary areas won by LCP, in dozens of acts of celebrations performed throughout the country, ”says the note.

According to the document, LCP 2024 calendars, decorated with images of the Palestinian National Resistance and the Revolutionary Area Tiago dos Santos, were also sent to Portelinha. They highlight the heroic resistance of the peasants in the revolutionary area who, in the 2021 battles against attempts at eviction, faced a war operation that “frustrated the attacks of the far -right government of the genocide Bolsonaro and the same generals who arrested and torture Alípio De Freitas ”.

The event aired Tiago Afonso's film “The Cause and the Shadow” and featured musical performances by artists Luanda Cozetti and Bruno Fonseca.

Alipio de Freitas was an important revolutionary and Portuguese journalist. Throughout his life, he was an important leader of the peasant alloys in Brazil, and became secretary of organization of the organization. During the period, Alipio formed important paintings of the peasant fight and was editor of Jornal das Ligas, The league . After the 1964 military coup, Alipio did not abandon the fight and went on to clandestinity. He even lived in Cuba, where he planned his return to Brazil. Back in the country, he was arrested in 1970 and was brutally tortured by the military regime gorillas. Torture did not heat it. In the 1980s, he worked in Agricultural Cooperatives of Mozambique, at the time directed by the then revolutionary Liberation Front of Mozambique (Frelimo) and, until his death, Alipio continued active in political militancy. In 2010, he participated in an expanded meeting of the Poor Peasant League Commission in Goiânia, and in the same year joined the Editorial Board of AND .

In connection with the 106-year mark of the Finnish working revolution and the boycott campaign against the presidential election, our comrades in Punalippu have posted an action report that we summarize here.

On January 27, Finnish Maoists, as well as representatives of the Communist Labor Party (KTP) and the Marxist Labor Association (MTL) gathered at the Kalevankanka cemetery in Tampers to honor the class heroes of the Workers Revolution in 1918. Flowers and lights were placed on the grave of the fallen red guardians, followed by the International and speeches.

The Maoists held the first appeal that dealt with the events and experiences made during the Finnish worker revolution, in addition, it took up the necessity of rejecting reformism and embracing the armed struggle. After the mark on the burial ground, the revolutionaries went to the center of Tampers where a demonstration train was held against the bourgeois electrical circus. Several appeals were also held on the central square. The Maoists raised much of the same points they had in the appeal at the burial ground, but also added how none of the presidential candidates oppose the Zionist state of Israel. They quoted Lenin there in his time have been talking about how limited the bourgeois democratic dictatorship actually is. It was also concluded here with powerful slogans.

The Marxist Labor Association again followed up with a speech about the ever deeper crisis of imperialism, NATO's function as a world police and the ever -increasing misery of the Finnish masses. It ended with a strong call to revolution to overthrow the old Finnish state.
